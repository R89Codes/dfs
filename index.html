Why DFS and BFS Match Asymptotically

They scan the same vertices and edges, but maintain different frontier structures.

DFS marks each vertex once and scans each adjacency list once, so its work is O(V + E). BFS does the same counting with a queue, so it is also O(V + E). The difference is not growth order; it is the search pattern and the frontier data structure.
